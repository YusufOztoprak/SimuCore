SimuCore is not just a game; it's a biological simulation engine driven by real-world ecological mathematics. Here is how the magic happens:

1. Allometric Scaling (Kleiber's Law)

Instead of guessing stats for animals, we use Allometric Scaling. Biological rates scale with body mass ($M$) according to power laws.

$$ R \propto M^{0.75} $$

Metabolic Rate ($R$): Larger animals consume more energy overall, but are more efficient per gram of tissue.

$$ r \propto M^{-0.25} $$

Reproduction Rate ($r$): Smaller animals reproduce significantly faster. For example, a mouse reproduces exponentially faster than an elephant.

2. Logistic Growth (Plants)

Plants follow the logistic growth model, limited by space and resources (Carrying Capacity, $K$).

$$ \frac{dN}{dt} = rN \left(1 - \frac{N}{K}\right) $$

Where $N$ is the population, $r$ is growth rate, and $K$ is the maximum capacity.

3. Lotka-Volterra Equations (Predator-Prey)

The core of the simulation models the cycle of life and death between hunters and the hunted.

Prey Equation (Herbivores)

$$ \frac{dH}{dt} = rH - aHP $$

Prey ($H$) grow at rate $r$, but are eaten by Predators ($P$) at rate $a$.

Predator Equation (Carnivores)

$$ \frac{dP}{dt} = bHP - mP $$

Predators ($P$) grow by eating Prey ($b$ is efficiency) and die naturally at rate $m$.
